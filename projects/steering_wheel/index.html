One of my passions is building new things, with my 3D printer I am able to create anything that I can imagine, and one day, just staring at a potentiometer I thought, "Um, maybe there is some way to build a steering wheel out of one of those?". And from that thought, this project born, made with a 3D printer, an arduino, a potentiometer, and some wires, it came to live.

What I learned

The first thing I got better was in CAD, where I use fusion 360 to be able to model all the parts I needed. With everything modeled, I had to 3D print them, another skill I got to get better, with this projects, I was able to play around with infill patterns and percentage as well as supports.

One thing that I was pretty bad (and I still am :P) is soldering, which was something I had to work on as well in this project, soldering the arduino pro micro pins was really a challenge for me, but, after 3 pins wasted and lots of tries, I got it right :D

The last thing was just assembling everything, using zip ties to pack the wires tightly, securing the potentiometer in place and coding it. For the coding part I used C++ with the library Joystick.h, that really accelerated my project, with around 30 lines of codes and some tries it was already working.

Also, this project is a mark because it's the first video I ever post on youtube, I don't know how this channel will turn out, I just posted the video this week but I hope to inspire more people to join this incredible field that is Engineering.
